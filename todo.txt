"Root cause of the ~1 second latency: every request to the protected mux (including this POST) goes through authMiddleware, which calls bcrypt.CompareHashAndPassword unconditionally on every single request — Basic-Auth credentials are resent by the browser on every request, and there's no session/cookie caching of a successful auth. WebUIPasswordBcryptCost has an enforced floor of 12 (sanitizeAndValidateConfig clamps anything below 12 back up to 12), and bcrypt cost 12 typically costs on the order of ~150–300ms per compare on ordinary hardware — that's an unavoidable per-request cost with the current design regardless of localhost networking. The full round-trip on top of that (POST → 303 redirect → full GET → full HTML reparse/DOM rebuild) doubles the network round-trips and adds a full page re-render, which is what's actually fixable here. A proper fix for the bcrypt-per-request cost itself would be an HttpOnly session cookie issued after a successful Basic-Auth, avoiding bcrypt on subsequent requests — that's already flagged in your own todo.txt as a distinct, larger, deliberately-deferred task, so I haven't touched auth in this response. What I am changing below removes the redundant round-trip and gives per-button optimistic UI so several clicks can be in flight independently." - Claude Sonnet 5 Extra Thinking
